How to prepare fried rice

Ingredients

  • 3¾ cups (750g) long grain parboiled rice
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Chicken (whole chicken or chicken drumsticks)
  • 100g cow liver
  • 1 tablespoon Nigerian curry powder (NOT Indian Curry)
  • ½ cup green beans
  • 3 carrots
  • 3 onions
  • 3 stock/boullion cubes
  • 1 tablespoon thyme

Steps

  1. Wash all the vegetables to be used in cooking the fried rice
  2. Scrape and cut the carrots into tiny cubes.
  3. Cut the green beans into small pieces of about 0.7cm long.
  4. Soak these 2 vegetables separately in hot water for about 5 minutes and drain.
  5. Cut 1 bulb of onion. Set all these aside.
  6. Prepare the liver and chicken
  7. Prepare the liver and chicken
  8. Cut the whole chicken into pieces and cook with the Knorr cubes, thyme and 2 bulbs of onions (chopped).
  9. When done, grill in an oven or deepfry with vegetable oil

Cooking Directions

  1. Parboil the rice using the method detailed in parboiling rice for cooking fried rice.
  2. Rinse the parboiled rice with cold water and put in a sieve so all the water drains out
  3. Pour the chicken stock into a sieve to remove all traces of onions, thyme etc used in cooking the chicken.
  4. Pour the stock into a pot and set to boil.
  5. Once the water boils, add the parboiled rice.
  6. Also, add 1 tablespoon of plain yellow curry powder, then add salt to taste.
  7. Stir the contents; cover the pot and leave to cook on medium heat.
  8. Once the water has dried up, the rice should be cooked perfectly.
  9. Now, transfer the rice to a casserole dish or another pot to help cool it down quickly.
  10. Again, this is essential to keep the grains from sticking to each other. If left in the original hot pot, the rice will continue to cook and eventually stick together.
  11. At this point, it is advisable to divide the vegetables into say 4, 5 or even 6 equal parts
  12. The rice will be fried in batches so this number depends on the quantity of rice you can comfortably fry in the pan or pot.
  13. Also, divide the cooked rice and the diced cow liver into the same number of equal parts.
  14. Now pour a small amount of vegetable oil into a frying pan. This quantity of oil should be such that it would be absorbed by 1 part of the cooked rice and 1 part each of the vegetables.
  15. When the oil is hot, add 1 part of diced onions and stir for 10 seconds, followed by 1 part of diced cow liver, one part each of the diced vegetables, then 1 part of cooked rice.
  16. Add more salt if necessary.
  17. More curry powder may also be added at this stage if you need to touch up on the colour.
  18. Stir till all the ingredients have mixed well and transfer to a dry pot.
  19. Repeat this for the remaining batches of the ingredients.
